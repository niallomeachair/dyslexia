Audio books

I found using audio books and podcasts to be invaluable in helping me to develop my understanding of what dyslexia is and how it was a power for good when properly understood. Many people with dyslexia who get to adulthood without knowing their disability have become more in tune with other areas in life. This is a development as a result of coping mechanisms to work around feeling or looking silly and begins at an early age.

Check yourself

If you suspect you might have dyslexia, consider the following signs and symptoms that are commonly associated with dyslexia in adults:

  • Difficulty reading, including slow reading speed and frequent misreading of words.
  • Problems with spelling, including frequent spelling errors and difficulty remembering how to spell common words.
  • Challenges with writing, such as difficulty organizing thoughts on paper and frequent grammar mistakes.
  • Difficulty with time management and organization, including trouble keeping track of appointments and deadlines.
  • Problems with memory, including difficulty remembering names, dates, and other important information.
  • Difficulty following multi-step instructions or directions.
  • Struggles with learning new languages or accents.
  • Difficulty with math, including trouble with basic arithmetic and understanding mathematical concepts.
  • Problems with concentration and focus, including being easily distracted.
  • Difficulty with sequencing tasks or events in the correct order.
  • Challenges with social interactions, including difficulty interpreting social cues and understanding sarcasm or humor.
  • Feeling overwhelmed or anxious in situations that require reading or writing.
  • Low self-esteem or feelings of frustration related to academic or work performance.

If you identify with several of these signs, it may be beneficial to seek a professional assessment from a psychologist or educational specialist who can provide a formal diagnosis and recommend appropriate support and accommodations.
