Think different

Dyslexia is often associated with unique strengths and abilities that can lead to innovative thinking and problem-solving skills. Many individuals with dyslexia are able to think outside the box and approach challenges from different angles, which can be a significant advantage in various fields.


Some of the strengths commonly associated with dyslexia include:

  • Creative thinking: Many individuals with dyslexia have a natural ability to think creatively and come up with innovative ideas.
  • Problem-solving skills: Dyslexics often excel at finding solutions to complex problems, as they are used to thinking in non-linear ways.
  • Spatial reasoning: Many people with dyslexia have strong spatial reasoning skills, which can be beneficial in fields such as art, design, and engineering.
  • Big-picture thinking: Dyslexics often have the ability to see the big picture and understand how different pieces fit together, which can be valuable in strategic planning and decision-making.
  • Resilience and perseverance: Many individuals with dyslexia develop strong resilience and perseverance as they navigate challenges related to their learning differences.
  • Empathy and emotional intelligence: Some dyslexics develop heightened empathy and emotional intelligence, which can enhance their interpersonal skills and relationships.
  • Oral communication skills: Many individuals with dyslexia excel in verbal communication, storytelling, and public speaking.
  • Entrepreneurial spirit: Dyslexics often possess an entrepreneurial mindset, driven by their ability to think differently and take risks.
  • Holistic thinking: Many individuals with dyslexia are able to see connections and patterns that others may miss, leading to a more holistic understanding of complex issues.
  • Innovative problem-solving: Dyslexics often approach problems in unique ways, leading to innovative solutions that others may not consider.
